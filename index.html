About

About me

I'm a data scientist with a background in economics and analytics, focused on building end-to-end ML systems — from exploratory analysis to deployed, monitored models in production.

My work sits at the intersection of rigorous statistical thinking, business acumen, and pragmatic engineering. I care about reproducibility, interpretability, and making sure results are actually used by the people who need them.

I did my undergrad at the University of Michigan, where I studied Economics with minors in Math and Religion. I also graduated with a Masters degree in Data Science from Michigan's School of Information, where I was invited to serve part time as a teach in the program.

Currently open to full-time roles in data science and machine learning.
